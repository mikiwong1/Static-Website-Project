Spots in NYC

NYC is a diverse place with many exciting places to visit. In this page, we will be focusing on three main attractions located in Manhattan. There are many more exciting spots to see in all 5 boroughs which are not mentioned here. However, all the activities listed below you can visit for free and easily accesible.

Central Park

The first is Central Park located between 59 and 110th street and 3rd and 8th avenue. It is a free park where you can enjoy many scenic views that change throughout the seaasons. One of these activities are bike riding which the park can let you rent out bikes to your own use. There is also a pedicab tour in case you would rather sit back and relax while enjoying the view of nature. Walking tours are also a great option to get moving and get to take your time sightseeing. Since, Central Park is so big, there are many scenes to view and you can plan your trip there to more than one time.

The High Line

Next up is the High Line which modernized an old railroad track into a park. It stretches from West 34th Street and 12th Avenue making it 1.45 miles long. This activity can take just 30 minutes if you decide to rush through the path. If you are well-versed on plants and insects this attraction maybe right for you. Especially during the summertime when flowers are blooming, you can take advatange of recording all the varitey of plants you come aross on the route. They have free tours on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Saturdays which offer new insights into the park and its history.

Bryant Park

Our last activity is Braynt Park. Although it is open all year round, during the wintertime, they open up their ice skating rink. With Christmas coming up soon, it is a fun hobby to take up with your friends.
